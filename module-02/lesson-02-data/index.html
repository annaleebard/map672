Our World

... an Interesting Yet Static Global Image

Pickles introduces us to an audacious and mystifying theory of the Cartographic Gaze. The gaze absurdly believes it is omnipotent and has the power (or genius) to scale this knowledge into map form. We all believe this to an extent; both the mappers and the mapped. I belive retaining neutrality while mirroring our world, as mappers, is impractical. I belive retaining neutrality while living in our world, as humans, is impractical. The gaze is inherent and the "ordering principles"" that filter all perceptions are as well. Pickles reminds us that objectivity in mapping is unattainable and, in my opinion, that's okay.
